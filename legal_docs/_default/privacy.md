# Privacy Policy

**This is a placeholder privacy policy shipped as the FreedomLS default. Replace it with your own legal copy before going live.**

## 1. Information we collect

When you create an account we collect:

- The email address you sign up with.
- Your first name (and last name, if you supply one).
- A record of your acceptance of these documents, including:
  - The version and exact text of the Terms and Privacy Policy you accepted.
  - The date and time of acceptance.
  - **The IP address you used at the moment of acceptance.** This is collected and stored as legal evidence of consent under data-protection regimes such as GDPR and POPIA.

We may also collect information you supply during onboarding (for example, additional registration form fields configured by the site operator).

## 2. How we use your data

- To operate the platform and deliver the educational content you have signed up for.
- To communicate with you about your account.
- To maintain a defensible record of your consent to these legal documents.

## 3. Retention

Consent records, including the IP address captured at acceptance, are retained for as long as your account exists, and for a further period after account closure to satisfy legal-evidence obligations. **The default retention period is 7 years; site operators MUST adjust this to match their jurisdiction's requirements before relying on this template.**

Other personal data is retained only as long as needed to operate the service and to satisfy applicable legal obligations.

## 4. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, export or erase the personal data we hold about you. Contact the site operator to exercise these rights.

## 5. Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with service providers that the platform relies on to operate, and only to the extent needed for them to provide their service.

## 6. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. Where the change is material, we will ask you to re-consent.
