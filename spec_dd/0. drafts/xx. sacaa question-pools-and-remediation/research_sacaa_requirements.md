# Research: SACAA / ICAO Requirements for Learner Remediation in Aviation E-Learning

Date: 2026-04-24
Scope: What SACAA actually requires (or SACAA inherits from ICAO) regarding
remediation after a failed knowledge check in aviation e-learning / CBT.

## TL;DR

- **No explicit SACAA regulation was found that mandates "active remediation"
  after an incorrect answer in formative (practice) quizzes.** SACAA rules
  that were located focus on *examinations* (pass mark, retakes, waiting
  periods), not on the learning loop inside an e-learning course.
- **The regulatory hook for remediation is indirect** and flows through:
  1. SA-CAR Part 141 / SA-CATS 141 requiring ATOs to deliver training that
     prepares the learner to pass the SACAA theoretical examination, and
  2. ICAO's Competency-Based Training and Assessment (CBTA) framework
     (Doc 9868 / PANS-TRG, Doc 9941), which SACAA as an ICAO contracting
     State is obliged to implement.
- In CBTA, **formative assessment with feedback is a required design
  property of the training system** — not just "nice to have". The
  "active remediation loop" feature aligns with this expectation, but the
  exact wording "active remediation" is **industry/instructional-design
  terminology, not a SACAA statute quote**.
- Below, every "requirement" claim is labelled either **[Verified]** (the
  source was read), **[Reported]** (repeated by secondary sources but
  primary text not confirmed in this research session), or
  **[Unverified]** (could not be confirmed — treat as best practice, not
  as law).

## 1. SACAA Regulatory Framework (what was confirmed)

### 1.1 Regulations that govern training

- **SA-CAR Part 141** (Civil Aviation Regulations, 2011, as amended) and
  **SA-CATS Part 141** (Technical Standards) govern Aviation Training
  Organisations (ATOs) in South Africa. The 21st amendment (Government
  Gazette No. 11359, Vol. 677, 15 Nov 2021) completely replaced Part 141,
  and SA-CATS 2/2021 (5 Nov 2021) completely replaced SA-CATS 141.
  **[Verified via SACAA training page]**
- **SA-CAR Part 61** covers personnel licensing of pilots, including the
  theoretical-knowledge examination framework. **[Verified]**
- **SA-CATS 61.01.10** is the technical standard that carries the
  examination procedure detail (subjects, formats, invigilation,
  notification rules). **[Verified — referenced directly on the SACAA
  Examinations page]**

### 1.2 Examination rules that *were* confirmed

These are examination-level rules, not in-course remediation rules, but
they define the boundary condition the learning loop has to feed into.

- **Pass mark: 75%** for SACAA theoretical-knowledge examinations.
  **[Reported by multiple secondary sources; confirmed as the industry
  norm for SACAA Part 61 exams. Primary-text confirmation not achieved
  in this session.]**
- **Retake waiting period: 7 calendar days** between attempts in the
  normal case. **[Reported]**
- **Extended waiting period: 60 days** if (a) the candidate has failed
  three times in a row, or (b) scored below 50%. **[Reported — this is
  the specific clause that is most relevant to "remediation": a very low
  score is treated as a signal that the candidate needs more learning
  time, not just another test attempt.]**
- Remark window: 30 days; only available for scores 70–74%.
  **[Reported]**
- A "5-day rule" under SA-CATS 61.01.10 paragraphs 2(f) and (g) governs
  notification when changing exam date/subject. **[Verified — referenced
  on SACAA Examinations page; not directly about remediation.]**

### 1.3 What was *not* found

Searches for the following specific terms in SACAA primary documents
returned **no direct hits**:

- "active remediation"
- "knowledge deficiency" (as a SACAA-defined term)
- a SACAA-level specification for what an e-learning course must do when
  a learner answers a formative quiz question incorrectly
- a SACAA-level distinction between formative and summative assessment
  inside an ATO's courseware

This does **not** mean there is no such clause buried in SA-CAR 141 or
SA-CATS 141 — the SACAA PDFs of those documents could not be text-
extracted in this session. Flagged as **[Unverified — needs primary-
source read]**. The next step if we want a definitive answer is to
obtain the 2021 SA-CATS 141 text and search it for "remedial",
"deficiency", "formative", and "feedback".

## 2. ICAO Framework (what SACAA inherits)

South Africa is an ICAO contracting State. SACAA is expected to give
effect to ICAO Standards and Recommended Practices (SARPs), and — where
national regulation is silent — ATOs are typically assessed against ICAO
guidance.

### 2.1 Annex 1 — Personnel Licensing

- Sets the licensing floor for pilots, FEs, dispatchers, ATCs, mechanics,
  etc., including theoretical-knowledge subjects. It is the origin of the
  "knowledge areas" SACAA tests in Part 61 exams. **[Verified via
  secondary sources; not directly about remediation inside a course.]**

### 2.2 ICAO Doc 9868 — PANS-TRG

- PANS-TRG is the procedural companion to Annex 1. Amendments 2, 3 and 7
  progressively pushed the industry toward **Competency-Based Training
  and Assessment (CBTA)**, Evidence-Based Training (EBT), and UPRT.
  **[Verified via ICAO and SKYbrary secondary sources.]**
- Under CBTA, **the training system itself is required to include a
  feedback process**: "The training system performance is measured and
  evaluated through a feedback process that uses training metrics to
  collect the post-delivery training program data." **[Reported from
  secondary sources summarising PANS-TRG; exact paragraph number not
  captured in this session — the ICAO PDFs would not text-extract.]**
- CBTA draws an explicit distinction between:
  - **Formative assessment** — used to monitor learning progress during
    training and provide continuous feedback to both trainee and
    instructor concerning learning success and *failure to meet
    standards*. **[Reported — IATA "Competency Assessment and Evaluation
    for Pilots, and Instructors/Evaluators" guidance material.]**
  - **Summative assessment** — at defined points or at the end of
    training; decision is "competent / not competent". **[Reported —
    same IATA GM.]**
- Key implication for the feature: **CBTA expects formative feedback to
  *identify gaps as learning opportunities* — not just to mark right/
  wrong.** This is the closest ICAO-level hook for an "active remediation
  loop". **[Reported]**

### 2.3 ICAO Doc 9941 — Training Development Guide (TDG)

- Defines ICAO's **Instructional Systems Design (ISD)** methodology:
  analysis, design, production, evaluation. **[Verified via ICAO course
  catalogue and secondary sources.]**
- ISD is the process by which an approved ATO builds courseware and
  validates it. Remediation after an incorrect formative-quiz answer is
  a standard ISD deliverable, but Doc 9941 is framework guidance, not a
  prescriptive rule. **[Verified that Doc 9941 exists and is the ISD
  reference; specific remediation-loop wording was not extracted.]**

### 2.4 Evidence-Based Training (EBT)

- Extends CBTA for recurrent pilot training. EBT's whole purpose is to
  detect and *close* individual competency gaps rather than tick off
  manoeuvres. **[Verified via IATA EBT Implementation Guide secondary
  material.]** Relevant to "active remediation" philosophically but not
  directly applicable to initial e-learning quizzes.

## 3. Formative vs Summative — practical impact on the feature

Based on the CBTA distinction above, the feature should likely behave
differently in two contexts:

| Context | Classification | Remediation behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| In-course practice quiz / knowledge check | Formative | **Active remediation loop expected**: explain why the answer was wrong, point the learner back to the relevant content, let them try again. Aligns with CBTA's formative-assessment definition. |
| End-of-module or end-of-course test gating progression | Could be summative at ATO level | Feedback per item may be deferred; the system still has to record the attempt. CBTA still expects the *overall* training design to feed identified gaps back into the learner's plan, but per-question "try again" is not necessarily appropriate. |
| SACAA Part 61 theoretical examination | Summative (regulatory) | **Out of scope for the LMS.** SACAA runs these at accredited test centres. The LMS does not "remediate" during a SACAA exam; the regulatory remediation mechanism is the 7-day / 60-day retake window. |

This three-tier model is a reasonable implementation stance and is
**consistent with** (not mandated by) the CBTA framework. **[Reasoned
inference, not a direct quote from a SACAA document.]**

## 4. Answers to the specific questions asked

### 4.1 SACAA regulations referencing remediation / deficiency correction in CBT / e-learning

- **None found at the statute level** in this research session. SA-CAR
  Part 141 / SA-CATS 141 govern ATOs but their text could not be
  directly extracted here; a primary-source read is still outstanding.
  **[Unverified]**
- The closest SACAA-level "remediation" hook that *was* confirmed is
  the **60-day cool-down after 3 fails or a <50% score** on theoretical
  exams (SA-CATS 61-area). **[Reported]**

### 4.2 ICAO guidance SACAA inherits

- **Annex 1** (licensing floor). **[Verified]**
- **Doc 9868 PANS-TRG** — CBTA mandates a feedback process and formative
  assessment as design properties of training. **[Reported]**
- **Doc 9941 TDG** — ISD methodology; courseware is expected to be
  designed with validated instructional components including feedback
  after incorrect responses. **[Verified existence; exact remediation
  wording not captured.]**
- **Annex 6** (operations) and its training requirements are relevant
  for operator recurrent training but **not** the primary driver for an
  ATO LMS feature. **[Not directly researched in this session —
  flagged.]**

### 4.3 SACAA Part 61 / Part 141 references to assessment and deficiency correction

- Part 61 / SA-CATS 61.01.10 covers *examination* administration:
  pass mark, retake timing, subject lists, invigilation. **[Verified as
  the source document; specific clauses on waiting periods
  [Reported].]**
- Part 141 / SA-CATS 141 governs the ATO, including training records and
  curricula, but the specific language on in-course remediation was
  **not located** in this session. **[Unverified]**

### 4.4 Specific language

- "Active remediation" — **not SACAA/ICAO terminology.** It is
  instructional-design industry language. Using it in product copy is
  fine; using it in compliance claims is misleading.
- "Knowledge deficiency" — appears in FAA guidance (e.g. FAA Notice
  8900.325 "Remedial Training Guidance and Procedures") and generally in
  CBTA literature, but **was not confirmed as a defined term in SACAA
  primary documents.** **[Unverified for SACAA]**
- "Remedial training" — **confirmed as standard CBTA terminology**;
  referenced in IATA GM and FAA guidance; specific SACAA usage not
  confirmed. **[Reported]**
- "Formative assessment" / "summative assessment" — **defined terms in
  ICAO CBTA / IATA GM.** **[Reported]**

### 4.5 Distinction between formative and summative

- Yes, this is a **defined distinction in ICAO CBTA / PANS-TRG guidance
  (Doc 9868) and IATA CBTA GM.** See table in section 3. Formative =
  continuous feedback during training; summative = competent / not
  competent decisions. **[Reported]**

## 5. What this means for the feature

1. **Compliance framing:** Do **not** claim "SACAA requires active
   remediation." The honest framing is: "SACAA, via SA-CAR 141 and its
   adoption of ICAO CBTA principles, requires that ATO training be
   effective and that learners be prepared for the SACAA examination.
   Active remediation after incorrect formative answers is a
   well-established CBTA design practice that supports that requirement."
2. **Design implications confirmed by CBTA/ICAO:**
   - Formative quizzes should give **specific feedback**, not just
     right/wrong.
   - Feedback should **direct the learner back to the source content**
     (consistent with ICAO ISD and CBTA "identify gaps as learning
     opportunities").
   - The system should **record** attempts and outcomes so that patterns
     of deficiency can surface — this is a CBTA "feedback process"
     expectation.
3. **Out of scope:** The SACAA theoretical examination itself. The
   regulatory retake logic (7 / 60 day rules) lives at SACAA, not in the
   LMS.
4. **Open item:** Obtain the full text of SA-CAR Part 141 (2021
   replacement) and SA-CATS 141 (Nov 2021 replacement) and search for
   "remedial", "deficiency", "formative", "feedback", "assessment".
   This is the only way to know whether SACAA has a prescriptive clause
   we are currently missing. This research session could not text-extract
   the relevant PDFs.
