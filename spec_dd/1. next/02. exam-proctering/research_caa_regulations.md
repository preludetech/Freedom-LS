# South African Civil Aviation Authority (SACAA) Exam Regulations Research

## Document Purpose

This research document summarises the SACAA regulations and requirements relevant to approved training organisations (ATOs) and their examination processes, with a focus on computer-based testing, proctoring, record-keeping, and exam administration.

**Primary Regulatory Sources:**
- **SA-CAR Part 141** -- Aviation Training Organisations (Civil Aviation Regulations, 2011, as amended)
- **SA-CATS Part 141** -- Technical Standards for Aviation Training Organisations
- **SA-CAR Part 61** -- Pilot Licensing
- **SA-CATS 61.01.10** -- Theoretical Knowledge Examinations (Technical Standard)

---

## 1. Examination Requirements -- SA-CATS 61.01.10

### 1.1 Examination Format

- Questions are held at the **Authority Central Question Bank** (SA-CATS 61.01.10(1)(b))
- Examinations are in **English only** (SA-CATS 61.01.10(1)(c))
- Questions are **multiple-choice** format with 3 or 4 choices. Variations include True/False, Yes/No, Multiple-response, and Fill-in-the-Blank (SA-CATS 61.01.10(1)(d))
- **No negative marking**: marks are not deducted for incorrect selections (SA-CATS 61.01.10(1)(e))

### 1.2 Online vs Written (Paper-Based) Examinations

SA-CATS 61.01.10(1)(f) specifies exam delivery modes:

| Location | Format | Exam Types Available |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| SACAA offices | Online | All examinations |
| Accredited Examination Centres (ATOs) | Online | PPL, PPL validation, Night Rating, Restricted Radiotelephony |
| Out-station venues | Written (paper) | CPL, ATPL, Instrument Rating, General Radiotelephony, Grade III FI Rating |
| Approved Part 141 ATOs | Authority-approved exam | SA Air Law for foreign PPL validation (without IR) |

### 1.3 Pass Mark

**75% pass mark** applies to all theoretical knowledge examinations.

### 1.4 Credit Time Limits

- All required examination subjects must be completed within **18 months** from the date of achieving the first credit
- Failure to complete within 18 months requires **rewriting all subjects**
- Once a subject is passed, it **cannot be re-sat** to improve marks while the credit is valid
- Skills test must be completed within **36 months** from passing all required subjects

## 2. Exam Retakes, Waiting Periods, and Failure Conditions

### 2.1 Retake Waiting Periods (SA-CATS 61.01.10(9))

**For PPL, Night Rating, and Restricted Radiotelephony:**
- **7 calendar days** after date of failure (no escalation for multiple failures)

**For all other licences/ratings (CPL, ATPL, IR, General Radiotelephony, FI):**
- **1st or 2nd failure**: 7 calendar days after date of failure
- **3rd or subsequent failure**: 2 calendar months after date of failure
- **Score below 50%**: 2 calendar months after date of failure (regardless of attempt number)
- The 2-month period is a **"compulsory retraining period"**
- The administrative official must verify eligibility for rewrites before scheduling

### 2.2 Remark After Failure (SA-CATS 61.01.10(10))

- Application for remark within **30 days** of result notification
- Only candidates who failed with **70% or above** may request a remark
- Cannot rewrite a subject while a remark is pending

## 3. Exam Proctoring, Invigilation, and Security

### 3.1 Invigilator Requirements

- **Grade 12/Matric Certificate** minimum education
- **Police Clearance** from SAPS
- **Signed Code of Practice/Conduct**
- **Invigilator training** conducted via online platform
- Approval valid for **5 years**
- An invigilator may invigilate at **no more than 5 exam centres**

### 3.2 Candidate Identification (SA-CATS 61.01.10(4))

- Candidates must arrive **at least 45 minutes** before examination
- Required: Original SA Identity Document or passport, Flight Crew Licence, Booking confirmation
- For initial writing: **proof of training** signed by the Chief Ground Instructor

### 3.3 Examination Room Security (SA-CATS 61.01.10(4)-(6))

**Prohibited:** bags, mobile phones, electronic devices, personal notepaper, headgear/earphones
**Permitted:** Non-programmable calculators, drawing/calculating instruments

### 3.4 Misconduct (SA-CATS 61.01.10(7))

- Disqualification from future examinations for up to **36 months**
- Deemed to have failed the examination
- Subject to enforcement action under Part 185

## 4. Record-Keeping Requirements

### 4.1 ATO Record-Keeping (CAR 141.01.17)

- **Detailed student records** showing all course requirements have been met
- Student records retained for minimum **5 years** after completion
- Instructor/examiner records retained for minimum **5 years**
- Training Procedures Manual must describe record completion and retention methods

### 4.2 Examination Records

- All candidates must sign the official **attendance register**
- Online results available **immediately after completion**
- Results never given over the telephone

### 4.3 ATO Graduation Certificates (CAR 141.01.22)

Must include: ATO name/certificate number, trainee name, course, graduation date, statement of satisfactory completion, Chief Instructor certification, unique sequential numbering.

## 5. e-Learning Requirements (Part 141 Subpart 7)

ATOs wishing to conduct e-learning must have this capability specified in their approved OpSpec. Detailed technical requirements are prescribed in SA-CATS 141.
